Comprehensive Analysis of Amazon Sales Discounts

by Joyce Mirador

Category: Python
Client: Personal Project
Project date: 18 June 2024
Project URL: Python file


In the ever-evolving e-commerce industry, understanding discount strategies can significantly impact sales performance and customer satisfaction. This analysis dives into Amazon's sales discount data to uncover patterns and insights that could guide more effective pricing strategies. By examining the relationship between discount percentages, sales volume, and product ratings, this analysis aims to provide actionable recommendations for enhancing discount efficacy.



Objectives

  • Determine Discount Patterns Across Categories: Calculate and compare the average discount percentage for each product category.
  • Analyze the Impact of Discounts on Sales Volume: Assess the correlation between discount percentage and rating count, and identify any discount thresholds that lead to a significant increase in rating counts.
  • Evaluate Discount Effectiveness on Product Ratings: Analyze the average product rating across different discount ranges (e.g., 0-10%, 10-20%, etc.) and determine if there is an optimal discount range that results in the highest average ratings.
  • Identify Successful Discount Strategies of Top-Rated Products: Examine the discount percentages of products with ratings of 4.50 and above, and compare these strategies with those of products with ratings below 4.00.


⃟⃟⃟


Data Preparation

The dataset, obtained from Kaggle, includes over 1,000 Amazon product ratings and reviews collected in January 2023 by J. Karkavelraja. Key features for this analysis include discount_percentage, rating, ating_countr, and category, with rating_count representing sales.

The data cleaning and transformation were done using Python. Checks for duplicate values revealed none while missing values were addressed by removing incomplete entries. Conversion of relevant columns to appropriate numerical data types facilitated accurate analysis. Further transformation included categorizing ratings into 'High' (product rating of 4.50 and above), 'Medium' (product rating of 4.00 to 4.50), and 'Low' (roduct rating of 4.00 and below) categories. This thorough data-cleaning process ensured the integrity and reliability of the dataset, allowing for the extraction of meaningful insights.

⃟⃟⃟


Analysis Results

Correlation and logistic regression analysis confirmed weak relationships among discount percentage, category, rating, and sales (rating count). Regression models showed low R-squared values, indicating that discount percentages do not significantly impact ratings or sales.

Key Findings:

  • Discount Patterns: High discount percentages were prominent in specific categories like Cable Connection Protectors and Earpads.
  • Impact on Sales Volume: The highest sales were noted in the 60-70% discount range, but overall, sales did not consistently increase with higher discounts.
  • Effectiveness on Ratings: Product ratings remained stable across various discount ranges, suggesting no optimal discount range for higher ratings.
  • Top-Rated Products: High-rated products (4.50 and above) did not exhibit distinct discount strategies compared to lower-rated products.


⃟⃟⃟


Recommendations

  1. Strategic Discount Planning: While higher discounts (60-70%) correlate with increased sales, excessively high discounts should be avoided as sales do not proportionally increase beyond a certain threshold. Optimizing discount percentages within the 20-70% range is recommended to balance profitability and sales volume.
  2. Enhanced Data Collection: Collect additional data, including seasonal trends, monthly and yearly reviews, and timestamps for product postings and discount implementations. This comprehensive data can reveal patterns that influence discount effectiveness over time.
  3. Accurate Sales Metrics: Obtain accurate sales data rather than relying on rating counts as a sales proxy. It will provide a more precise understanding of the impact of discounts on actual sales behavior.
  4. Advanced Analytical Models: Utilize other machine learning models to better understand the relationships between discount percentages, ratings, and sales. It could uncover evident patterns and provide actionable insights for refining discount strategies.

This analysis reveals that while discounts play a role in sales and customer engagement, their direct impact on ratings and overall sales volume is limited, indicating the need for a more accurate approach to discount strategies.
